AUDIO

This is my interpretation of the sound textures that should be implemented in the short film 'Behind Closed Doors' by flash fiction writer, Margaret Cahill.

All sounds were either downloaded or field recorded using a ZOOM H6 recorder. The final render above was edited using Logic and Audacity.

Certain effects like muffling the outside sounds when the toilet door is closed, were created by using EQ techniques. To achieve this, frequencies in the higher range were cut uding a graphic equiliser in Logic.

PRODUCTION

Other techniques that were used to add a sense of movement were the automation of panning, from left to right and cross-fading volumes that overlapped each other. This gives the listener a sense that there is movement taking place as the sound shifts from one ear to the other.

Some subtle reverb effects were added in places to create a haunting sound, like where the baby is crying.

The finished film can be viewed on the video page of this website and was completed as part of a group work project.

SOUND

As part of a small group, we were required to select a location where we could take a short walk around. During this walk we would record and make note of all sounds that stood out to us within the environment. See the image below for a reference to our sound walk map.

WALK

The creative aspect is that the outcome of our soundwalks is to use the collected or experienced sounds to create collaborative improvisations, as part of a socially engaged sound art practice, enabling yet another level of reflection about soundscapes. - softday.ie
