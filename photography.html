The concept of affordance as described by Don Norman relates to the psychological relationship between objects and how we as actors interact with them. Objects, by their very nature, establish a relationship with us through their composition. We instinctually guage their materiality, whether they are solid, comfortable, sturdy, empty, heavy, transparent. The ever changing relationship we maintain with objects allows for designers to use and manipulate concepts involved with affordance to inform elements of our designs.

Affordance Photography

Below are some interactive thumbnail images that display the before Photoshop and after Photoshop results. The original images were taken in RAW format which allows a higher dynamic range to work in while editing the originals. A jQuery plugin called before&after by Catch My Fame was used to create the slider that allows transitioning between each image by interacting with the slider.

Slide RIGHT for before photoshop and slide LEFT to see the photoshopped image. Some images only have subtle changes to brightness, levels, curves or exposure. Remember, slide RIGHT for before photoshop and slide LEFT to see the photoshopped image. If the slide function does not work on your tablet, you can still tap the image to animate the before and after slider across the image.

Head Stock

The guitar head stock provides a number of affordances. The tuning pegs afford twisting in both directions.

The original image was a bit dull and had to be brightened up slightly. The contrast and exposure were increased in photoshop also.

Shaving Socket

The bathroom shaving socket, used here to charge an electric toothbrush, affords the toothbrush charger to hang from the socket.

The original image was taken while the incandescent light in the bathroom was on. The image had to be colour corrected in photoshop.

Sitable

The wall affords the gentlemen a comfortable perch on which to sit and chew the fat. The wall is 'sitable'.

Again, using Photoshop, the image was brightened and the contrast level was increased.

Asemblage

Three spring loaded items that afford, pressing, squeezing, pinching and clamping.

I felt that the original image was a bit on the dull side. In order to rectify this I increased the exposure level slightly. When I look at it now, the photoshopped image looks totally over-exposed in the white areas.

Vase & Door Knob

The vase has a hole that affords the opportunity to put items into. I put the door knob in!

The original image was crooked so it had to be cropped and straightened in Photoshop. Also some adjustments were made using curves and levels.

Clothes Peg

The shape of the peg suggests that it should be pressed together at one end.

The contrast was increased in order to make the peg the centre of focus. The image is over exposed in general.

Remember Slide RIGHT for before Photoshop and slide LEFT to see the Photoshopped image. The slider handle can be dragged or simply click the image to move it.

Pipe Stat

The cable tie clamps the pipe thermostat to the the pipe. The stat has a grooved bracket at the back to allow it to fit to the contours of the pipe.

The original image was under exposed and quite noisy. Photoshop was used to brighten the overall image and levels and curves were used to increase the orange colour of the stat. A lens blur effect was applied to blur the background and make the sta pop.

Business Call

The trailor affords the gentleman a perch on which to sit and take a break on a hot day. He can rest his legs on the ladder structure.

A lens blur effect was used in PS to attempt a tilt shift effect. The area surrounding the man is blurred while the character is kept in focus. This draws the eye towards him as he should be the centre of focus.

“Good design is actually a lot harder to notice than poor design, in part because good designs fit our needs so well that the design is invisible,” ― Donald A. Norman, The Design of Everyday Things

Tweezers

The tweezers affords the opportunity to squeeze between the fingers. The black plastic pads offer a comfortable place for fingers.

The original image, before Photoshop, was totally overexposed. Photoshop was used to reduce the exposure and increase the contrast which makes the detail of the tweezers much clearer.

Tuner

The clamp on the tuner is spring loaded and affords the opportunity to clamp the tuner to a guitar head stock or other objects.

The original image had to be brightened and the contrast had to be increased. This was done in Photoshop alongside some other adjustments to levels and curves.

The image effects and transitions were provided through several jQuery and javascript plugins and custom code. The plugin used for the image gallery is Galleria, a free responsive javascript image gallery framework. The before and after image transition animation is provided through before&after a jQuery plugin that offers this effect. In order to successfully get the slider plugin to work , it was also neccessary to incorporate the use of jQueryUI . This allows the dragging interaction to be available for the plugin.
