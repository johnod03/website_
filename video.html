Behind Closed Doors

Behind Closed Doors on Vimeo.

Video Production

We were required to form groups of up to 4 members and choose from a pool of short stories written by Margaret Cahill.

The stories were quite dark and we could interpret them as we liked.

The story chosen by our group was called Behind Closed Doors. It had quite a dark theme.

Process

We were asked to create screen plays and scripts before any shooting would begin. Click on this link to see the final draft of our screen play written by Conor Souness.

Our story had a dark theme so we wanted to use this as a tonal aspect in our version........

The next stage of our process was to create some storyboards. These would help to visualise the shots and their composition. It also helped to spot, early on, if there would be any glaring mistakes made with respect to how the story would take shape. Clodagh drew up some storyboards and upon reviewing them, the team was then on the same page, so as to speak.

Clodagh secured some actors from the drama society who agreed to play the 2 main roles in the movie. The storyboards were used to explain what type of roles they would be playing and how the shots would look.

My main roles in the movie were as the director of photography, camera man and editor. Gary Whelan also acted as the main camera main for all of the location shooting. The editing was all conducted in Adobe Premier Pro. Some of our biggest challenges were with lighting and shooting outdoors at night. We had to be careful of our own shadows creeping into the shot.

I was also responsible for the titling which was produced in Premier Pro. The vast majority of the editing involved getting timings right and some shots had to be satbilised. Stabilisation was achieved by opening the Premier Pro comp in After Effects as an After Effects composition. This way we could experiment with effects in AE and as soon as the AE comp was saved, it would update in Premier Pro. This was an ideal work flow.
