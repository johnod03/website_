PROTOTYPING

Prototyping is a great way of experimenting with ideas and testing out techniques before heading straight into development of a project. Prototypes can be tested, iterated, refined or disgarded if necessary. The beauty of a low fidelity prototype is that it should cost very little but explore the relevant area as thoroughly as possible. I began my prototyping phase on paper using mind-mapping techniques, brainstorming and quick lo-fi iterative design. I then moved on to creating video prptotypes. I believe that, now, I have taken my prototypes to a place where they can be tested on a user base and refined further.

The prototype above is not interactive just yet. I could use a wizard of OZ technique when I bring this to user testing sessions. I did some very basic experimenting with video sonification for this iteration. The sonification techniques used in this involve both synthesis and MIDI combined. I have used arbitrary mappings for now but for sonification of videos to take place, I think that the mappings need to be given a good deal more consideration. As it stands, the mappings are reletively non-deterministic and the prototype has led me to believe that they should be more deterministic in nature. Perhaps this is somethign that can be brought to user testing sessions.

Initial

After some sketching of ideas and brainstorming, I decided that some video prototypes needed to be created. These would help me to get an understanding of what a video installation would look like potentially. The next set of videos demonstrate, roughly, what my concept could look like.

Neither of these initial prototypes are 'sonified' as their purpose is to give a feel for visuals. Nor are they interactive although the audio is interacting with the video content.

The above video prototype implements a split screen effect. It would be possible to route each video feed to its own projector and beam 2 individual large scale projections using this approach.

Prototypes

The video prototype below attempts to give a visual representation of what a number of randomly placed, randomly sized video frames would look like if I were to go with this approach. I haven't ruled this out and will surely use this in my user testing sessions. I look forward to user feedback regarding this.

The video prototype in the demo video above experiments with different approaches as to how the feeds could be displayed. I played around with a single frame that fills the projection area then a split screen. At this stage of development it is worth trying out as many differnt layouts as possible.

Although, I have a MaxMSP patch created that loads and plays random videos from a folder on my hard drive, the prototypes are created using Adobe After Effects and Premiere Pro. I recorded the video and the audio generated from the Max patch and imported them into Premiere for some post production. This included trimming, fading and volume adjustment.

Sonified Prototype 1

The video prototype below was the first attempt at video sonification. It is a 2 minute video demonstrating a Max patch that randomly loads 2 videos at a time, displays them symmetrically and sonifys them based on luminosity and average HSL colour values.

The values that are retrieved from the video data currently power to audio components. The first is an oscbank~ object in Max, the second is a MIDI engine that sens MIDI data to Ableton Live wher a drone instrument is used.

The oscbank~ object works quite well with the luminosity values especially when ther is a dramatic change of light in a video feed. The use of synthesis would be my preferred way to go with this installation but getting the mappings right is crucial to the success of the project.

Below are some notes and sketches illustrating some examples of brainstorming sessions, mind-mapping and paper prototyping. In order to get to the bottom of what I am trying to do or want to do even, I found the process of sketching any ideas that came to mind to be imperitive to getting to the 'heart of the matter'. I often refer back to my notes and each time I come across something that has slipped my mind. Seeing my thought pattern develop from what might have originally just been a splurge of scribbles and sketches is a most useful endeavour.

I put a lot of time and work into creating these video prototypes and I'm glad I did. Along the way I decided that it would be a good idea to begin researching sonification techniques and try to implement them, somehow, into my prototypes. If done well enough, I feel that they would help to explain exactly what I wanted to achieve.

I also believe that it is important in this case, at is a video installation, that a more functional prototype would benefit me greatly. I hope I am correct regarding this thought. That remains to be seen, I guess.
